Chocolate Cake

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature it can reach (usually around 475°F to 500°F or 245°C to 260°C).
  2. Prepare the pizza dough by combining 2 ¼ teaspoons of active dry yeast, 1 ½ cups of warm water, 1 teaspoon of sugar, and 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a mixing bowl. Let it sit for 5 minutes until the yeast is activated and foamy.
  3. Gradually add 4 cups of all-purpose flour and 1 ½ teaspoons of salt to the yeast mixture. Stir until the dough comes together, then transfer it onto a floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 to 1 ½ hours, or until it doubles in size.
  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two equal portions. Roll out each portion into a desired pizza shape on a floured surface.
  6. Transfer the rolled-out dough onto a baking sheet or a pizza stone. If using a baking sheet, lightly grease it or line it with parchment paper.
  7. Spread your favorite pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Then add your desired toppings such as cheese, vegetables, meats, and herbs.
  8. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for about 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  9. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. This will help the toppings set and make it easier to slice.
  10. Slice the pizza into wedges or squares, serve, and enjoy your homemade, piping hot pizza!

Note: Feel free to experiment with different toppings and flavor combinations to suit your preferences. Bon appétit!
